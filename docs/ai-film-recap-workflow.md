# AI Film Recap Workflow

Film recap is harder than standard clipping because the short depends on story coherence, not just isolated highlights. A good film recap needs setup, progression, tension, and payoff, even when compressed into a very short runtime.

## What Makes Film Recap Different

A film recap workflow usually fails when it treats the source like a quote-mining exercise. The recap needs to preserve narrative logic, which means the editor has to identify story turns, not just memorable scenes.

Key challenges:

- too much source material
- plot dependency between scenes
- tone consistency
- character motivation getting lost in compression
- recap turning into random scene stitching

## A Practical Workflow

### 1. Define The Recap Type

Choose one recap mode before editing:

- full plot recap
- character arc recap
- high-tension recap
- twist-focused recap
- fast summary for discovery content

This keeps the recap from becoming a shapeless overview.

### 2. Map The Narrative Spine

Before cutting, identify:

- setup
- inciting event
- first major turn
- rising conflict
- peak moment
- resolution or cliffhanger

If a scene does not help one of those beats, it usually does not belong in the first draft.

### 3. Reduce Scene Count Aggressively

Strong recap content usually needs fewer scenes than people expect. More scenes often make the recap harder to follow because context gets thinner with every jump.

As a rule of thumb:

- keep only scenes that change the story
- prefer scenes with visible stakes
- remove duplicate emotional beats

### 4. Add Bridging Language

Film recap often needs short connective lines such as:

- "At first, it looks like..."
- "But that changes when..."
- "What he does not realize is..."
- "By this point..."

These lines do not just explain the story. They repair compression damage.

### 5. Decide What To Show And What To Tell

In recap work, some information is better narrated than shown, especially when:

- the source scene is visually weak
- the explanation is faster than the footage
- multiple events can be combined into one sentence

The best recap workflows use footage for emotional proof and narration for compression efficiency.

### 6. End On A Clear Payoff

A recap short should still close on something satisfying:

- a reveal
- a consequence
- a dramatic irony beat
- a twist
- a final emotional takeaway

Without that, the short can feel like a trailer instead of a recap.

## Quality Checklist

- Can a new viewer follow the plot spine?
- Is every scene carrying story weight?
- Are names, motives, and stakes clear enough?
- Does the narration bridge missing context?
- Does the recap feel shaped rather than stitched?

## Where AI Helps Most

AI is most useful in film recap when it helps with:

- scene segmentation
- plot summarization
- rough script drafting
- subtitle generation
- first-pass pacing suggestions

AI is least useful when the recap still needs high-level judgment about tone, emphasis, or what emotional reading the short should produce.

## Final Takeaway

Good film recap is not about including more of the movie. It is about protecting the narrative spine while removing everything else.
