# Film Recap Example

## Source

- Source type: long-form narrative video
- Goal: 60-second recap short
- Angle: tension and payoff

## Weak Draft

"This story is about a man who lives alone and starts noticing strange things around him. At first he does not think much of it, but later..."

Problem:

- slow opening
- vague setup
- low urgency

## Stronger Draft

"He thinks he is being watched, but the real twist is that he has already missed the only chance to get out."

Why this works better:

- starts closer to the payoff
- creates immediate tension
- gives the viewer a reason to stay

## Structure

1. Hook with the threat
2. One line of setup
3. Two escalating story turns
4. Peak moment
5. Final consequence

## Editing Note

The recap gets stronger when scene count is reduced and short bridging narration is added between major turns.
